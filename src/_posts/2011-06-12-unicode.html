"Unicode Senki" (Senki is "war chronicle" in Japanese) is an interesting documentary about Unicode and standarization. This book was written by Kobayashi Tatsuo, ex-JustSytems and the co-director of the Unicode Consortium.

"Defined by the specification" is acutually "Defined by someone wrote the specification". This book shows how standarization folks struggle the difficulity and how Japanese tackle an international project. Really impressed.

Last year I watched the mailing list of OAuth carefully because we need to release new product with OAuth 2.0 based authorization. But I didn't join the discussion. My ex-boss did it on OpenSocial.

Sometimes my coworker said "it's a WebKit/Fx's bug. I can't fix it.". It's little frustrated for me because they divide the world very easily. Divide and conquer? Well, but if you find a bug, you get a chance to contribute the community. It's really interesting experience.

However I should see that I did't tackle the specification (or the standarization) yet. Some of caused by my English skill, but some is not.
