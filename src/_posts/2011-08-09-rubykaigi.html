Better late than naver. I will write an last entry about RubyKaigi.

ucnv - Visual Glitch, using Ruby

Maybe, glith is fresh for Ruby programmers. But I think it's not so minor. For example, Kanye West used it on his video.

ucnv talked about a definition, techniques and his libraries: AviGlitch and PhotoShop.

Andrew Grimm - Finding Black Holes in Ruby with the Small Eigen Collider

Andrew talked about Small Eigen Collider. I think SEC is a kind of fuzzing. So I asked on the session: Why don't you generate whole Ruby code? His answer is clear: Because it's difficult and too complex. I got it. We can generate whole Ruby code, but it's not easy to report the difference between implementations. SEC is more pragmatic way to do it.

Inoue Makoto - The Great Eastern Japan Earthquake and what you can do as a Rubyist?

Makoto talked about sinsai.info, Ushahidi-based site for the earthquake. Fukushima is my home. Maybe I shoud do something, but I didn't do anything yet...

Akita Fabio Makto - Personal Dilemma: How to work with Ruby in Brazil?

Akita talked about his challenge - how to work with Ruby in Brazil. But his talk is not tied to Ruby. He talked "how to adopt good technology outside the States". His talk and passion is really impressive.

Asari Hirotsugu - Know Ruby, will travel

Hiro talked about "how to work in foreign countries". His advice is not easy, but pratical.

Matsumoto Yukihiro - Pendulum, PG and the hundred year language

And Matz! His talk was really fun.

  • In Japan, many programmers (even young programmers) write own language.
  • It's crowded market, or Kodoku. (Note: most Japanese don't know Kodoku)
  • And Ruby is the dominant, best player in the market. If you make new language. I will crush you!

Some programmers are great, some programmers (ex. Tatsuhiko Miyagawa) is cool, and Matz is nice. I know.
