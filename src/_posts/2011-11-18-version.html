--- title: How to versionize own fork of CPAN modules? layout: post ---

Regexp::Optimizer 0.15 doesn't work well with Perl 5.14. Yesterday I fixed and bumped up the version to 0.15_google. Well, "google" is just an example. I mean, I added a suffix to distinguish our version from the original. However I couldn't use the version on Perl. 

% perl -Mversion -e 'warn version->parse("0.15_google")'
Invalid version format (non-numeric data) at -e line 1.
%

I thought a little and found a leet solution to the problem. 

% perl -Mversion -e 'warn version->parse("0.15_900913")'
0.15_900913 at -e line 1.
%

How cool? but many companies can't use the trick.

We need a new version specification for Carton era

I think Carton will give us the definitive way to manage dependencies on Perl applications. Then version.pm should provide more expressive way to distinguish many versions.

rpm, dpkg and Semantic Versioning allow to use alphabet on version numbers. Why don't we follow the predecessors?
