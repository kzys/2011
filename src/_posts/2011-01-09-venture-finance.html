I read Isozaki Tetsuya's "Venture Finance - The Essential Guide for Entreprenuers". The author's writing style is too casual for me. But the content is interesting and practical.

Personally, I like a small – Bootstrapped, Profitable, & Proud – startup. 37signals's "Rework" has a chapter called "Outside money is plan B". Today, Apple, Google and Amazon provide a "store" and a payment service for individial developers. Amazon also provide EC2, S3 and CloudFront. We don't need a large company for a software business.

However, "We are small" is not a good reason to ignore finance, I think.
